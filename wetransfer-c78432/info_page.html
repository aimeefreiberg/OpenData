The Poverty Investment Index (PIV) was designed to combine many different factors into one single index to help you make the best decision on where your investment will have the highest impact to reduce poverty. Currently, 5 factors influence this index: poverty, ease of business, the gini index, gender equality, and number of children out of school. The index ranges from 0 (investments are not effective) to 10 (investments are very effective).



The Index Factors

Poverty

The poverty data is the poverty headcount ratio at national poverty lines (% of population). The National poverty headcount ratio is the percentage of the population living below the national poverty line(s). National estimates are based on population-weighted subgroup estimates from household surveys. As the aim is to reduce poverty, it is crucial that high poverty rates strongly influence the index.


Ease of Business Index

Ease of doing business ranks economies from 1 to 190, with first place being the best. A high ranking (a low numerical rank) means that the regulatory environment is conducive to business operation. The index averages the country's percentile rankings on 10 topics covered in the World Bank's Doing Business. The ranking on each topic is the simple average of the percentile rankings on its component indicators. As poverty is best reduced by supporting people in establishing their own income to become self reliant, their ease of doing so was therefore included. In countries with high ease of business financial investments are more effective.


The Gini Index

 Gini index measures the extent to which the distribution of income (or, in some cases, consumption expenditure) among individuals or households within an economy deviates from a perfectly equal distribution. A Lorenz curve plots the cumulative percentages of total income received against the cumulative number of recipients, starting with the poorest individual or household.A Gini index of 0 represents perfect equality, while an index of 100 implies perfect inequality. Sharing of resources is a vital aspect of improving living conditions for many people.


Gender Equality

Gender equality assesses the extent to which the country has installed institutions and programs to enforce laws and policies that promote equal access for men and women in education, health, the economy, and protection under law.


Children Out of School

Children out of school are the number of primary-school-age children not enrolled in primary or secondary school. Investment in education is vital in supporting a more promising and steady future.

Generating the Poverty Investment Index

For each factor, the mean of the last 5 years of data (2014 - 2015) was calculated. If no data was available in the last 5 years, the last recorded entry was taken. If there was no data at all on the country, the average of the factor of all other countries was used as not to lose the country in the index calculations. These factor values were then normalised (set between 0 and 1) and adjusted so that 1 encourages investing and 0 discourages investing. This data was then combined with the following formular.

PIV = 2 * Poverty + Ease of Business + The Gini Index + Gender Equality + Children Out of School

If a focus is selected (such as gender equality or children out of school) the factor will be weighted twice as strongly as before within the index.



Investment and the Influence
on Poverty

Foreign direct investment can contribute in significant ways to the breaking of the poverty circle. Contrarily is argued that FDI also contributes to a "race to the bottom", where countries are forced to lower their standards and wages so as not to lose investments and jobs. In a study it was found, that FDI has a positive but not significant impact on real per capita income and hence does have the potential of reducing poverty in the country. Therefore care should be taken when attracting foreign investments and they should be directed to the productive sectors of the economy. We have chosen poverty, per-capita income and economic growth as the three factors to show possible impacts of FDI on poverty reduction over time.
